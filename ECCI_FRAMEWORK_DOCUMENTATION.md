## Common Questions & Answers

### Q: "Won't simplifying the language reduce our academic credibility?"

**A:** No. Accessibility and credibility are not mutually exclusive. You're still using terms like "neuroscience-backed," "research-based," and "proprietary framework." You're just explaining them in terms of **benefits** rather than **processes**.

---

### Q: "Should we reveal the 16 specific frameworks?"

**A:** No. The specific frameworks remain proprietary. However, you should always reference that they are:
- Research-backed (credibility)
- Integrated (comprehensive)
- Proven effective (results-oriented)

---

### Q: "How do we balance 'for exhausted interpreters' with 'professional and educated'?"

**A:** Your audience IS professional and educated, which means:
- They can understand neuroscience concepts
- They appreciate research-backed solutions
- BUT they don't have time for academic jargon
- They need **solutions**, not **theory**

**The approach:** Explain like you're talking to a colleague, not a researcher.

## Style Guide: ECCI™ Framework Language

### Preferred Phrases

### When to Use Technical Language

**Use technical language when:**
- Writing for academic audiences
- Submitting grant applications
- Publishing research papers
- Speaking at conferences

**Use accessible language when:**
- Creating marketing materials
- Writing website copy
- Designing user onboarding
- Developing sales materials
